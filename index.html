The problem

USCIS sent the RFE on Monday. Your client called Wednesday. You opened the file Thursday. The deadline is in 3 weeks.

Every RFE is a 20-page research project disguised as a government letter. Your paralegal spends 8 hours gathering evidence, cross-referencing policy memos, and drafting the response. Meanwhile, 6 other cases are waiting. One missed deadline and your client's case gets denied. The bottleneck isn't legal knowledge. It's the time it takes to assemble the response.

01

USCIS requirements change without announcement. A form field that was optional becomes required. A supporting document category that was accepted is now consistently flagged. You follow the same checklist you have used for years, and an RFE arrives on a case you thought was clean. You are not making mistakes — the requirements moved.

02

Responding to a single RFE takes 6-12 hours: reviewing the notice, locating the missing evidence, drafting the response brief, coordinating with your client. For a solo attorney billing 40-50 cases a year, 3-4 RFEs a year is 24-48 hours of unplanned work. That is a full week of your capacity, absorbed by cases you thought were done.

03

Your clients hired you because they trust you to navigate the system correctly. An RFE is not just a bureaucratic delay — it is a moment of visible failure in front of a client who is waiting to change their life. You know it is not your fault when USCIS moves the goalposts. They do not know that.

How it works

Upload the petition package. RFEGuard flags what USCIS will flag.

Step 1

See your RFE response drafted automatically, with evidence citations ready

Upload the petition, all supporting evidence, and the cover letter. RFEGuard reads the complete package against the petition type, visa category, and current USCIS adjudication standards — not a static checklist, but dynamic policy tracking.

Step 2

Know exactly which supporting documents are missing and where to find them

RFEGuard identifies every evidence category that USCIS requires or consistently requests for your petition type. Missing tax returns, outdated form versions, insufficient specialty occupation support — every gap flagged before it becomes an RFE.

Step 3

Meet every USCIS deadline without burning out your paralegal team

RFEGuard monitors USCIS policy memoranda, AAO decisions, and adjudication trend data. When requirements shift in a category you file in, you get a notification before the change costs you a case. No immigration checklist tool does this today.

What We Learned

01

The ICP exists but the search volume doesn't

Solo immigration attorneys filing H-1B petitions are a real, reachable ICP. But they don't search Google for RFE prevention tools — they rely on AILA listservs, peer referrals, and existing practice management software. Google Ads was the wrong channel.

02

IntakeLink captured the adjacent demand

Our sister bet IntakeLink (immigration client intake automation) is converting at $18 CAC on the same ICP segment. The intake pain is more searchable and more acute than the RFE prevention pain.
